- [Q0] What is the most critical "deadly mistake" an ethical hacker can make before starting a test?

  Answer: Not getting prior approval in writing
- [Q1] When obtaining documented approval, what must be included?
  Options: ['A list of all passwords', "The hacker's home address", 'The plan, schedule, and systems to test', 'A guarantee to find all vulnerabilities']
  Answer: The plan, schedule, and systems to test
- [Q7] What does a ""good ethical hacker"" know?
  Options: ['Everything about information security', 'Their limitations and where to get answers', 'Only how to use one specific tool', 'How to bypass the law']
  Answer: Their limitations and where to get answers
- [Q18] How should an ethical hacker view the target network?
  Options: ["From a manager's viewpoint", "From a hacker's viewpoint", 'Only from the internal network', 'From a non-technical perspective']
  Answer: From a hacker's viewpoint
- [Q30] Which systems should an ethical hacker focus on?
  Options: ['Standalone desktops with no network card', 'The most expensive hardware', 'Systems and operations that matter most', 'Only the oldest systems']
  Answer: Systems and operations that matter most
- [Q42] What is a risk of ""pounding production systems"" at the wrong time?

  Answer: It could take down critical systems at the worst moment
- [Q54] If you outsource security testing, what is your responsibility?
  Options: ['To stay involved throughout the entire process', 'To hand over everything and forget about it', 'To never talk to the third party', 'To do the work yourself instead']
  Answer: To stay involved throughout the entire process
- [Q67] 100 percent, ironclad security is easily attainable if you have a large budget.
  Options: ['True', 'False']
  Answer: False
- [Q114] You should obtain documented approval and a signature agreeing not to hold you liable for unintentional bad things.
  Options: ['True', 'False']
  Answer: True
- [Q115] Ethical hacking is a one-time event that does not need to be repeated.
  Options: ['True', 'False']
  Answer: False
- [Q129] Is it possible to find all vulnerabilities during a security test?

  Answer: No, because so many known and unknown vulnerabilities exist
- [Q141] Regarding security vulnerabilities, which of the following is true?
  Options: ['100 percent, ironclad security is attainable', 'Maintaining 100 percent security is not attainable', 'One test is enough to eliminate all risks', 'Patching and hardening systems is useless']

- [Q153] Why must ethical hacking tests be performed periodically?
  Options: ['To charge the client more money', 'Because new threats and vulnerabilities surface continually', 'Software never changes', 'To practice using new tools']
